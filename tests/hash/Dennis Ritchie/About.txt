Dennis Ritchie (1941 - 2011) was an American computer scientist. He is known for creating the C programming language and co-creating the Unix operating system. 

Biography:
Born in Bronxville, New York on September 9, 1941
Died on October 12, 2011 at the age of 70
Co-winner of the 1983 A.M. Turing Award, the highest honor in computer science
Retired as head of Lucent Technologies' System Software Research Department in 2007

Contributions:
Created the C programming language in 1972
Co-developed the Unix operating system with Ken Thompson
Co-developed the B programming language with Ken Thompson

Other information:
Ritchie was known to be a lifelong Christian who kept his faith mostly private.
Ritchie had a dry sense of humor and a keen appreciation for life's absurdities.
